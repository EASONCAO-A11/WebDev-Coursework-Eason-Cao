The design thinking process influenced my approach to web design by helping me focus on the needs of the user and client instead of only focusing on appearance. It encouraged me to plan carefully, gather feedback, test ideas, and improve my designs through iteration. The process helped me better understand how design decisions affect the user experience and made my projects more organized and purposeful.

One example of how I applied the design thinking process was during the creation of my portfolio website. First, I identified the main goal of the website, which was to showcase my projects and skills in a clear and professional way. During the ideation stage, I brainstormed layout ideas, color schemes, and navigation styles. After creating an initial version of the website, I asked classmates and users for feedback about readability, design, and navigation. Based on their suggestions, I improved the layout, added more spacing, adjusted colors, and included more visual elements. This process helped me create a website that was more visually appealing, easier to navigate, and better suited to the needs of users.
