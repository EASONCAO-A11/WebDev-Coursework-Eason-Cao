During the brainstorming process, I explored different ideas for how my website could look and function. 
I created a simple idea board to organize my thoughts, including possible color schemes, layout ideas, and content sections.

I considered using a clean and simple design with a limited color palette, focusing on readability and easy navigation. 
I also thought about how users would move through the website, so I planned to include a clear navigation bar and organized sections such as Home, About, and Projects.

I sketched a basic layout of the homepage to visualize where elements like the header, navigation bar, and content sections would be placed. 
This helped me better understand how the website would be structured before starting to build it.

One key takeaway from this process is that planning the design in advance makes it easier to build the website later. 
It also helped me focus on user experience by thinking about how users will interact with the site.
