In Unit 4, I gained a much deeper understanding of why accessibility is an essential part of modern web design. 
Before this unit, I mostly thought about how a website looks, but now I realize that a truly effective website 
must also be usable by people with different abilities, devices, and needs. Learning about accessibility tools 
such as screen readers and text‑to‑speech software helped me see how important it is to provide meaningful alt text, 
clear structure, and semantic HTML. These small details can completely change how someone experiences a webpage.

Adding images to my client page also became more intentional. Instead of just placing a picture for decoration, 
I learned to think about what information the image conveys and how it supports the user’s understanding. 
Writing descriptive alt text made me consider the purpose of each image and how someone who cannot see the picture 
would still understand the content.

Overall, Unit 4 taught me that accessibility is not an extra feature — it is a responsibility. 
When a website is accessible, more people can use and engage with the content, 
and the design becomes more inclusive and thoughtful. Moving forward, I will continue to 
evaluate my pages with accessibility in mind and make sure every design choice supports a wider range of users.
